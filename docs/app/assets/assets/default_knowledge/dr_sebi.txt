=== SCIENTIFIC CONTEXT & CRITICAL NOTES ===

Dr. Sebi was not a medical doctor, pharmacist, or credentialed scientist. His work exists outside mainstream medicine.
These notes present relevant scientific context for informed evaluation.

WHAT SCIENCE SUPPORTS:
- Plant-based diets DO reduce dietary acid load (confirmed by research)
- Plant-based diets reduce risk of coronary heart disease by ~40% (peer-reviewed reviews)
- Plant-based diets cut metabolic syndrome and type 2 diabetes risk by ~50% (2017 review)
- Sea moss DOES contain significant mineral content: folate, vitamin K, B vitamins, iron, iodine, magnesium, zinc, calcium
- Sea moss may help lower bad cholesterol (LDL) and reduce blood pressure (Cleveland Clinic)
- Alkaline diets improve metabolic function markers (emerging research)
- Wild whole-plant foods have higher phytonutrient density than processed equivalents

WHAT SCIENCE HAS NOT CONFIRMED:
- No peer-reviewed research confirms the Dr. Sebi diet can cure AIDS, cancer, lupus, or sickle cell
- The claim that "all disease is one disease" is not accepted in scientific medicine
- The concept of "electrical" food charge has no validated measurement methodology
- The body maintains blood pH within a narrow range (7.35–7.45) regardless of diet through buffer systems
- No official health authority (FDA, WHO, NIH) has approved any Dr. Sebi compound as medicine

IMPORTANT NOTE ON IRON IN SPINACH:
- Science agrees: spinach contains oxalic acid which significantly reduces iron bioavailability
- Non-heme iron in spinach is only 2-13% absorbed (vs. 15-35% for heme iron from meat)
- Sebi's criticism of spinach iron has some scientific basis — though his categorization of spinach as "acid" (dietary pH) is not supported by standard pH measurement

NOTE ON THE COURT CASE:

- He was NOT acquitted on the basis that his cures were scientifically proven
- He was later successfully sued for consumer fraud (paid $900, agreed to stop disease claims in advertising)
